# Chapter 1: Introduction to Physical AI

Physical AI represents a paradigm shift in artificial intelligence, moving from purely digital intelligence to embodied intelligence that interacts with the physical world. Unlike traditional AI that operates in virtual environments, Physical AI requires systems that can perceive, reason, and act in real-world environments.

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI combines:
- Perception systems to understand the environment
- Reasoning capabilities to make decisions
- Actuation systems to interact with the physical world
- Learning mechanisms to improve over time

## Key Characteristics

1. **Embodiment**: The AI system has a physical form that interacts with the environment
2. **Real-time processing**: Decisions must be made within physical constraints
3. **Multi-modal sensing**: Integration of vision, touch, sound, and other sensors
4. **Action-Perception loop**: Continuous cycle of sensing, reasoning, and acting

## Applications of Physical AI

- Service robotics
- Industrial automation
- Assistive technologies
- Exploration robots
- Humanoid companions

## Challenges in Physical AI

Physical AI faces unique challenges:
- Safety in human environments
- Real-time performance requirements
- Hardware limitations
- Environmental uncertainty
- Complex system integration
